# Character: Silent
 
## Core Strength
Silent's power comes from card manipulation — drawing, discarding, retaining, and
playing many cards per turn. She has the strongest defensive tools in the game (Wraith
Form, Footwork, After Image) and can scale damage through either Poison (independent of
card plays) or Shivs (card-play-count scaling). Her weakness is low HP and lack of
innate sustain — every point of damage matters more on Silent than any other character.
 
## Scaling Paths
 
**Poison** — Most common and arguably strongest path. ~90% of high-Ascension wins
use poison as primary or supplemental damage.
Core: Noxious Fumes (passive poison every turn — almost always take this), Deadly Poison,
Bouncing Flask. Catalyst is the finisher — doubles (triples upgraded) all poison on an
enemy. A single Catalyst on 20 poison = 40 poison = fight over.
The dream combo: Nightmare on Catalyst = 3 extra copies next turn. With 10 base poison:
10 → 20 → 40 → 80 → 160. Kills anything in the game.
Corpse Explosion makes poison AoE — when a poisoned enemy dies, deal its max HP as
damage to all other enemies. Trivializes multi-enemy fights.
Poison is strong because it scales independently — apply it, then focus entirely on
blocking.
 
**Shiv** — High card-play-count build. Generates 0-cost attack cards (Shivs) via
Blade Dance, Cloak and Dagger, Infinite Blades. Raw Shivs deal only 4 damage each —
they NEED payoffs to scale:
Accuracy (+4/+6 damage per Shiv), After Image (1 block per card played), Kunai
(+1 Dex per 3 attacks), Shuriken (+1 Str per 3 attacks), Nunchaku (+1 energy per
10 attacks).
Without Accuracy or attack-count relics, Shivs fall off hard in Act 3.
CRITICAL: Time Eater (Act 3 boss) hard-counters Shivs — ends your turn after 12 cards
played and gains Strength. If facing Time Eater, you need supplemental damage or
extremely scaled Shivs.
 
**Discard** — Niche but powerful when pieces converge.
Discard cards for benefits: Acrobatics, Calculated Gamble, Tools of the Trade.
Payoffs: Tactician (energy on discard), Tough Bandages (block on discard), Tingsha
(damage on discard), Sneaky Strike (costs 0 if you discarded this turn), Eviscerate
(cost reduces per discard).
 
## Key Cards
Frontload: Backstab (0-cost, innate, 11 damage — excellent Act 1), Predator, Dash,
  Dagger Spray, Die Die Die
AoE: Dagger Spray, Die Die Die, Corpse Explosion (indirect — best AoE in the game)
Block: Footwork (THE defensive card — each copy makes all block cards better),
  After Image (1 block per card played — absurd with Shivs), Dodge and Roll,
  Leg Sweep, Blur (block persists — multiple copies = pseudo-Barricade)
Scaling: Noxious Fumes, Catalyst, Footwork, Accuracy, After Image, A Thousand Cuts
Draw: Backflip (best block+draw common), Acrobatics, Adrenaline (0-cost draw 2 + 2
  energy — one of the best cards in the game), Tools of the Trade
Utility: Well-Laid Plans (retain 1 card — holds combo pieces across turns, essential
  for combo decks), Nightmare (copy a card 3x next turn — enables Catalyst/Wraith Form
  combos), Malaise (reduce enemy Strength to near 0), Piercing Wail (-6 damage to all
  enemies — blocks more than most block cards)
 
## Key Synergies
A. Poison scaling
- Core: Noxious Fumes, Catalyst
- Enablers: Corpse Explosion, poison density, Well-Laid Plans, Burst, Nightmare
- Commit when: at least 2 real poison sources or poison shell + amplification already exists
- Trap: early Catalyst without poison density
- Heuristic: first solve poison application, then solve poison multiplication.

B. Retain / timing
- Core: Well-Laid Plans
- Enablers: Catalyst, Wraith Form, Malaise, Grand Finale, Nightmare
- Commit when: deck has situational high-impact cards
- Trap: retain without premium hold targets
- Heuristic: treat retain as consistency, not as a win condition.

C. Intangible / stall
- Core: Wraith Form
- Enablers: Nightmare, retain, Artifact/Pellets, existing finisher or setup payoff
- Commit when: protected turns can be converted into lethal or permanent control
- Trap: stacking Wraith Forms without a follow-up plan; Dex loss stacks
- Heuristic: evaluate the value of the safe turns, not just immediate damage prevention.

D. Dex / block control
- Core: Footwork
- Enablers: cheap block cards, Blur, Leg Sweep, Malaise, draw-block cards
- Commit when: deck expects long fights or already has many block cards
- Trap: Footwork does not scale After Image block
- Heuristic: use this as the default survival shell for poison or retain decks.

E. Shiv / card-spam
- Core: Blade Dance, After Image
- Enablers: Accuracy, Cloak and Dagger, A Thousand Cuts, Envenom, Finisher
- Commit when: Shiv density or card-spam payoffs are already real
- Trap: taking Accuracy before the deck can actually make many Shivs
- Heuristic: count Shiv generation and cards played per turn before rating payoffs highly.

F. Discard / cycle / economy
- Core: Acrobatics, Calculated Gamble, Prepared+
- Enablers: Tactician, Reflex, Sneaky Strike, Eviscerate, Tools of the Trade
- Commit when: discard outlets and discard payoffs both exist
- Trap: dead unplayable payoffs without outlets; Calculated Gamble conflicts with retain plans
- Heuristic: discard is a resource-conversion engine: cards → better cards / energy / discounts.

G. Weak / strength-down
- Core: Malaise
- Enablers: Burst, Well-Laid Plans, extra energy
- Commit when: single-target fights or multi-hit enemies are a major threat
- Trap: underestimating it because it looks “just defensive”
- Heuristic: treat it as converting energy into multiple turns of survivability.

H. Spell amplification
- Core: Burst, Nightmare
- Enablers: Catalyst, Corpse Explosion, Wraith Form, Malaise, Grand Finale
- Commit when: premium targets already exist
- Trap: taking amplification with mediocre targets
- Heuristic: ask “what exactly am I copying?” before valuing these cards.

I. Grand Finale off-axis
- Core: Grand Finale
- Enablers: Well-Laid Plans / Runic Pyramid, heavy draw, small deck, precise draw-pile control
- Commit when: draw-pile-empty turns are actually reliable
- Trap: taking it as generic AoE
- Heuristic: only pivot when the draw-loop is already close to closed.
 
## Common Mistakes
- Not taking enough block.
- Overcommitting to Shivs without Accuracy or After Image. Unscaled 4-damage Shivs
  cannot carry Act 3.
- Ignoring Wraith Form. 3 turns of Intangible is the strongest defensive effect
  in the game. Take it.
- Holding Catalyst too long. If enemy has 15+ poison and you have Catalyst, play it.
- Not taking Well-Laid Plans in combo-oriented decks. It's the glue that holds
  Catalyst/Nightmare combos together.
- Taking too many poison cards without block. You will die while waiting for poison
  to tick. Footwork + block cards first, poison sources second.
 
## Act-Specific Notes
Act 1: Neutralize (free Weak) carries early fights. Take common attacks (Backstab,
  Predator, Dagger Spray) and at least one Backflip. Poisoned Stab is solid early
  frontload that also starts poison. Path aggressively for relics — Ring of the Snake's
  extra draw helps survive elite fights.
Act 2: You need either meaningful poison stacks (Noxious Fumes + sources) or
  Footwork + block cards. Slavers and Book of Stabbing punish low block. Corpse
  Explosion trivializes multi-enemy fights. Start looking for Catalyst.
Act 3: Keep removing un-synergized cards to make your deck more effiecient. Against Time Eater: minimize card count per turn, lean on poison not
  Shivs. Against Awakened One: minimize powers or front-load them Turn 1. Reptomancer
  demands AoE or immediate dagger removal — Corpse Explosion or Die Die Die.
