# Character: Watcher
 
## Unique mechanic: Stances
Calm (gain 2 energy on exit), Wrath (deal and take double damage), Divinity (triple
damage + 3 energy, one turn only, requires 10 Mantra).
 
## Core Strength
Watcher is widely considered the strongest character in the game when played correctly,
and the most dangerous when played incorrectly. The Wrath stance doubles ALL damage —
both dealt and received. This means one mistake (ending turn in Wrath while enemies
attack) can instantly kill you. But correctly cycling Wrath → Calm → Wrath generates
enormous damage AND energy (2 free energy each time you exit Calm). Mastering stance
dancing is the fundamental Watcher skill.
 
## Key Cards
Frontload: Conclude (AoE finisher), Wallop (damage + block equal to damage dealt),
  Ragnarok, Wheel Kick
AoE: Conclude, Bowling Bash, Tantrum (hits random enemies)
Block: Wallop (attack + block), Talk to the Hand (gain block equal to attack damage on
  target — absurd in Wrath), Like Water (block in Calm each turn), Mental Fortress
  (block on stance change)
Scaling: Deva Form, Devotion, Battle Hymn, Rushdown (draw engine in Calm)
Draw: Empty Mind, Inner Peace, Rushdown (draw 2 on Calm enter), Scrawl (draw until
  hand full — 0-cost)
Utility: Vault (end turn, take an extra turn — one of the best cards in the game),
  Omniscience (play a card from deck twice), Lesson Learned (upgrade a card on kill),
  Wish (choose: Strength, Gold, or Plated Armor)
 
## Key Synergies
A. Stance-draw / Rushdown loop
- Core: Rushdown
- Enablers: Tantrum / Eruption, cheap Calm entry (Fear No Evil / Inner Peace / Vigilance / Tranquility), Empty Mind, Flurry of Blows, Violet Lotus
- Commit when: you already have repeated stance entry/exit and the deck is compact enough that draw actually closes loops
- Trap: taking Rushdown with too little stance density, or in a bloated deck where “draw 2” does not create a real loop
- Heuristic: rate this as Watcher’s highest-ceiling package when stance changes produce **net draw + net energy + safe exit**; do not just count Wrath entries, count whether you can leave Wrath cleanly too

B. Stance block shell
- Core: Mental Fortress, Talk to the Hand
- Enablers: Tantrum, Flurry of Blows, Wave of the Hand, Empty cards, Wallop
- Commit when: you already attack a lot per turn or stance-swap frequently enough that block is generated passively rather than by spending full cards on defense
- Trap: taking Talk to the Hand without enough attack density, or expecting one copy alone to solve all defense
- Heuristic: this is the default “I can safely abuse Wrath” shell; if the deck changes stance a lot, Mental Fortress scales defense automatically, and if it attacks many times, Talk to the Hand turns offense into block

C. Retain / cost-reduction package
- Core: Establishment, Meditate
- Enablers: Protect / Sands of Time / Windmill Strike / Smite generation, Battle Hymn, Vault, Scrawl, Runic Pyramid
- Commit when: you already have multiple high-impact retained cards or cards that become absurd when they reach 0 cost
- Trap: taking Establishment with too few retain sources, or in a deck whose value comes from cycling fast rather than holding cards
- Heuristic: this package is about **banking tempo across turns**; good when the deck wants to set up one explosive turn instead of spending energy efficiently every single turn

D. Divinity / Mantra burst package
- Core: Devotion, Prostrate / Pray / Worship, Blasphemy
- Enablers: Ragnarok, Wallop, Brilliance, retain, draw, Vulnerable support
- Commit when: you either have enough mantra density to enter Divinity predictably, or you already have one massive payoff turn that Blasphemy can convert into lethal
- Trap: taking weak mantra pieces one-by-one without a real payoff, or using Blasphemy in turns that do not actually end the fight
- Heuristic: this is a burst package, not a passive shell; only commit when Divinity meaningfully converts into kill pressure or a giant defensive swing

E. Big-turn / spell-cheat package
- Core: Omniscience, Scrawl
- Enablers: Vault, Devotion / Blasphemy, expensive payoffs, retained discounts, setup powers
- Commit when: the deck contains cards that are much stronger when cheated out or doubled than when played fairly
- Trap: taking Omniscience with no premium target, or taking too many expensive payoff cards before fixing setup consistency
- Heuristic: ask “what am I cheating, and does that win the fight or lock it up?”; Omniscience is not generic value, it is a multiplier for already-broken targets

F. Scry / consistency / Weave package
- Core: Cut Through Fate, Third Eye / Just Lucky, Weave
- Enablers: Nirvana, stance cards, low curve, reach-for-key-card decks
- Commit when: you already care about finding exact pieces on exact turns, or you have one or more Weaves that actually convert scry into damage
- Trap: treating scry as a win condition by itself; it is usually an engine-quality package unless Weave density is real
- Heuristic: scry is mainly a consistency multiplier — it smooths Wrath exits, finds combo pieces, and turns Weave from filler into recurring 0-cost damage

G. Fasting / debuff-cleanse package
- Core: Fasting
- Enablers: Artifact, Orange Pellets, Deva Form, energy relics, cheap efficient attacks/blocks
- Commit when: you can either negate the energy-loss debuff or comfortably operate on reduced energy because your card quality is already high
- Trap: taking Fasting in a clunky, expensive deck with no cleanse/artifact support
- Heuristic: treat Fasting like Watcher’s version of “quality over quantity” scaling — better cards per energy, but only if the energy problem is solved

H. Created-card package
- Core: Battle Hymn, Master Reality
- Enablers: Establishment, retain shell, Dead Branch, empty-hand / low-attack-count decks
- Commit when: created cards are a meaningful fraction of your output rather than just incidental value
- Trap: taking Master Reality with too few creation sources, or stuffing the deck/hand with generated attacks that interfere with cleaner stance loops
- Heuristic: this is a value package, not an auto-commit; it works when generated cards are both strong and actually castable on time

I. Pressure Points off-axis package
- Core: Pressure Points
- Enablers: retain, draw, stall, defensive shell
- Commit when: you already have multiple copies or the deck is clearly pivoting away from normal attack-based scaling
- Trap: mixing Pressure Points with ordinary Watcher attack packages and assuming they naturally support each other
- Heuristic: treat this as a deliberate branch; it scales through Mark and HP loss, so it wants survivability and repetition, not normal attack synergies

J. Alpha / Omega off-axis package
- Core: Alpha
- Enablers: draw, retain, Vault, Scrawl, stall, Omniscience
- Commit when: the deck can reliably survive and accelerate through the Alpha → Beta → Omega chain
- Trap: taking Alpha in a fast deck that already has better kill lines, or in a deck that cannot afford dead setup turns
- Heuristic: this is a long-fight inevitability package; only take it when your deck can pay the setup tax without bleeding out

K. Lesson Learned tempo package
- Core: Lesson Learned
- Enablers: frontload damage, retain, draw, stance burst, hallway-fight control
- Commit when: early enough that repeated upgrades materially change deck power, and you can reliably engineer fatal turns
- Trap: overwarping combat lines around forcing the upgrade when it costs too much HP or tempo
- Heuristic: this is not a full archetype; it is a growth package that raises deck quality over the run if the kill setup is cheap enough
 
## Common Mistakes
- ENDING TURN IN WRATH WHEN ENEMY IS ATTACKING. You take DOUBLE damage. This is the
  #1 cause of death. Always have a Calm exit before ending turn in Wrath.
- Ignoring Calm's energy. Exiting Calm gives 2 energy — entering Calm then exiting
  next turn is banking energy, not wasting a turn.
- Not taking Talk to the Hand. It solves offense AND defense in Wrath.
- Holding Mantra cards for a "big turn." Accumulate steadily — each Mantra card gets
  you closer to Divinity.
- Playing too many cards per turn against Time Eater. Watcher can easily play 12+
  cards — against Time Eater, lean on fewer high-impact plays (Wrath-doubled damage)
  rather than many small ones.
- Staying in Wrath for "more damage next turn." The risk almost never justifies it.
  Exit to Calm, re-enter Wrath next turn with the energy bonus.
 
## Act-Specific Notes
Act 1: Eruption + any Wrath attack kills most hallway enemies fast. Take Tantrum or
  Empty Fist for Wrath entry, Inner Peace or Empty Mind for Calm exit. Mental Fortress
  early is exceptional. Miracle (from Pure Water) helps with energy on Turn 1.
Act 2: You need consistent Wrath cycling or early Mantra generation. Talk to the Hand
  solves both offense and defense. Protect the Spire and Slavers are dangerous if you
  end turn in Wrath — always have an exit.
Act 3: Keep removing un-synergized cards to make your deck more effiecient.
Vault and Omniscience are game-winning if found. Scry and card draw help
  consistency. Against Time Eater, lean on fewer high-impact plays. Against Awakened
  One, Powers are fine (Watcher doesn't spam as many as Defect). Against Donu and Deca,
  Wrath cycling with AoE (Conclude, Bowling Bash) cleans up efficiently.
