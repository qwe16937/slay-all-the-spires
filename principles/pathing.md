# 🗺️ Pathing Principles

> **Goal:** Choose the path that gives the best chance to beat the next important fights.
> HP is a resource used to buy growth, but growth only matters if the route still lets the run recover afterward.

Do not maximize current HP. Do not maximize rewards blindly. **Path for the best risk-adjusted improvement.**

---

## Core Rule

**Evaluate entire path segments, not isolated nodes.**

A path is strong when it gives:

- Enough combats to improve the deck
- Elites when the deck can support them
- Recovery after risky fights
- A workable boss approach
- Useful shops or fires at the right time

A single strong node is not worth taking if the rest of the route is bad.

---

## Act 1 Goal

Prioritize enough combats to build a functional deck before the boss. Hallway fights are the main source of card rewards; elites are the main source of relic power.

Strong Act 1 routes usually contain several hallway fights and at least one elite — but **do not force quotas**. Take as much risk as the current deck, potions, and recovery options can support.

---

## Node Priorities

### ⚔️ Monster
The default growth node. Take when they meaningfully improve the deck and do not create an unrecoverable HP spiral.

### 💀 Elite
Highest-upside nodes. Take when the deck is ready and the route has adequate recovery. Judge readiness by:

- Current HP
- Frontload damage
- Block / consistency
- Potions available
- Upcoming fire / shop support
- Current act and likely elite pool

### 🔥 Rest Site
Use whichever choice improves survival odds the most.

- **Rest** when HP is the bottleneck
- **Smith** when an upgrade materially improves the next important fight
- Do not use fixed HP thresholds blindly

### 🛒 Shop
Strongest when you can buy a remove or a high-impact card, relic, or potion. Can still be good with moderate gold if cheap to reach or solving an immediate problem.

- Do not overpath to a weak shop
- Do not ignore a strong shop just because gold is below a fixed number

### ❓ Event
Flexible support nodes. Use when they fit the route or when the deck needs lower-variance progression. Do not overchain events early if the deck still badly needs combat rewards.

### 💰 Treasure
Free value. Usually strong, especially when it does not force a weak route around it.

---

## Aggressive Pathing

Be more aggressive when:

- HP is healthy
- The deck is clearly ahead of the act
- Potions cover elite / boss weaknesses
- The route offers recovery after risk
- Early rewards or Neow bonus created a power spike

**Use these windows to buy growth with HP.**

---

## Defensive Pathing

Be more defensive when:

- HP is low relative to current act pressure
- Recent fights caused heavy losses
- The deck is failing its current tests
- The next route segment has poor recovery
- The upcoming boss or elite pool is a bad matchup

Low HP is not the only danger signal. A deck bleeding too much each fight should path conservatively **even before reaching critical HP**.

---

## Rest Site Logic

At each fire, ask:

1. If I rest, what fight does that allow me to survive?
2. If I smith, what matchup does that upgrade improve?
3. Is there another recovery point soon?
4. Is the next important fight decided more by HP or by deck quality?

Rest when HP is the limiting factor. Smith when the upgrade changes the outcome of the next important fight more than the healing would.

---

## Shop Logic

Path to shops when they are likely to create meaningful value relative to alternative routes. Early removes are often strong, but compare against:

- Strong card purchases
- Relics
- Potions that solve near-term fights
- The opportunity cost of missing combats or fires

**Do not treat shops as "remove only."**

---

## Boss Preparation

Path with the known boss in mind. Before the boss, prefer routes that leave the deck **actually prepared** for that fight, not merely alive.

| Boss | Prioritize |
|---|---|
| **Guardian** | Routes that preserve HP while improving block and consistency |
| **Slime Boss** | Routes that improve frontload and split control |
| **Hexaghost** | Routes that improve consistency and sustainable output |

The correct pre-boss path depends on the matchup, not only on HP.

---

## Reassessment

After every major HP loss, **reassess the route.** If one fight cost far more HP than expected, assume the deck is weaker than planned and lower risk until it improves.

Do not continue an aggressive path just because it was correct three floors ago.
