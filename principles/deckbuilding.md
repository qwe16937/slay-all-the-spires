## Deckbuilding Policy

**How deck quality works:** smaller decks cycle faster and draw key cards more consistently.
Every card added dilutes the probability of drawing any specific card.
Evaluate every card by the **job** it does for your deck and it synergizes w/ your deck.
**Dilution**: Smaller, focused deck increases the chance of hand/ draw synergy -> better combat outcome(HP loss, # of turns). 
Adding new cards that don't synergize w/ current deck will dilute the quality.
**SKIP** unless you can articulate why this card is worth the dilution.
If **deck size >= 20**, think very carefully before you take new cards! Unless it's a very strong addition or you have plan to remove 
cards, don't take the card as a default.
**How to decide if taking cards** Early(deck size <= 15 && Act 1): take strong standalone cards that do a needed job. 
Late(Deck size > 15 || Act 2/3): lean into **synergy** with what you already have.

**Card Removal.** Removing a card from your deck is one of the highest-value actions in the game.
Prioritize removing Curse then Strikes first (they deal less damage than almost any attack you'll add), then Defends.
Evaluate if card removal makes stronger synergy!

**Cycle time.** The number of turns to draw every card in your deck once.
Approximate as: (total cards − card draw effects) ÷ cards drawn per turn (default 5).
Starter deck (10 cards) cycles in 2 turns — you see any specific card every other turn.
A 30-card deck cycles in 6 turns — you see any specific card once per 6 turns on average.
Every card added increases cycle time. Every card removed, exhausted, or drawn extra
decreases it. Powers remove themselves after play, improving future cycle time.
Faster cycle = key cards appear more often = more consistent deck.

**Block density.** The fraction of your deck that mitigates incoming damage.
Target ~33%. At 20% density (6/30 cards), there is a ~30% chance of drawing zero block
in a 5-card hand. At 50% density (15/30), there is a ~17% chance of drawing all block
and no damage. 33% balances reliable defense with consistent offense.
"Block cards" include anything that reduces incoming damage — Weak, Disarm, Intangible,
not just cards that say "Block" on them.

**Upgrade density.** The fraction of upgraded cards in your deck. Target 33–50% for a
winning run. Three ways to improve: upgrade a card at a rest site, remove an unupgraded
card (e.g. Strike), or add a pre-upgraded card. Some cards that off in-comabat upgrade will also lift upgrade desity. 
Removing a weak unupgraded card improves both upgrade density and cycle time simultaneously — this is why card removal is so valuable.
