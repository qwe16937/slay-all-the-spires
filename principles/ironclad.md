# Character: Ironclad
 
## Core Strength
Ironclad's defining mechanic is Exhaust — permanently removing cards from the deck
mid-combat. This is not just a niche archetype; at high Ascension, exhaust synergy is
the dominant strategy because it achieves both offense and defense simultaneously while
thinning the deck to its most powerful cards. Almost all successful Ironclad decks use
exhaust to some degree, even if they don't run the full engine.
 
## Scaling Paths (recognize, don't force)
 
**Exhaust Engine** — Highest ceiling, most consistent at high difficulty.
Core trinity: Corruption (all skills cost 0, exhaust on play) + Dark Embrace (draw on
exhaust) + Feel No Pain (block on exhaust). Once online, you cycle your entire deck per
turn, generating massive block and draw. Damage via Juggernaut (damage on block gain),
Body Slam (damage = current block), or simply overwhelming card advantage.
This path needs at least two of the three core pieces to function — one alone is weak.
Corruption without Feel No Pain or Dark Embrace will empty your hand and kill you.
Shrug It Off becomes 0-cost "gain 8 block, draw a card" under Corruption — take multiples.
 
**Strength Scaling** — Simplest path, fine default if exhaust pieces don't appear.
Stack Strength via Inflame, Demon Form, Spot Weakness, Limit Break. Deal damage through
multi-hit attacks: Sword Boomerang, Heavy Blade (3x Strength multiplier), Whirlwind,
Twin Strike, Pummel. Limit Break upgraded (removes Exhaust) enables exponential scaling —
upgrade it immediately if pursuing this path. Reaper (heal for unblocked damage dealt)
is the payoff card that recovers HP lost during setup. Demon Form is slow (3-cost) —
only take with energy support (4+ energy or Snecko Eye).
 
**Block/Barricade** — Powerful when assembled but a trap at high Ascension.
Barricade (block persists) + Entrench (double block) + Body Slam. Requires multiple
rares, slow to set up, and fails against time-pressure fights (Heart's scaling damage).
Only pursue if Barricade appears early with strong block generation (Impervious, Power
Through, Shrug It Off). Do not force this path — community consensus calls it a
"noob trap" at A15+.
 
**Self-Damage** — High risk, niche.
Rupture (Strength on HP loss) + Brutality (lose 1 HP, draw 1 each turn) + Hemokinesis /
Bloodletting / Offering. Requires Reaper as safety net. Only viable if Rupture appears
early and self-damage cards follow naturally.
 
## Key Cards
Frontload: Carnage, Pommel Strike, Headbutt, Clothesline, Uppercut
AoE: Cleave (early game), Whirlwind, Immolate (best AoE in the game for Ironclad)
Block: Shrug It Off (best common — always good, insane with Corruption), Impervious,
  Flame Barrier, Power Through (pairs with Second Wind)
Scaling: Demon Form, Inflame, Limit Break, Feel No Pain, Corruption
Draw/Utility: Offering (almost always correct to take — 3 cards + 1 energy for 6 HP),
  Battle Trance, Burning Pact, Pommel Strike (also key infinite combo piece)
Exhaust: Corruption, Dark Embrace, Feel No Pain, Fiend Fire, Second Wind, True Grit+
 
## Key Synergies
A. Exhaust engine
- Core payoffs: Corruption, Dark Embrace, Feel No Pain
- Enablers: Burning Pact, True Grit, Fiend Fire, Second Wind, Power Through, Sentinel
- Heuristic: if Corruption + (Dark Embrace or Feel No Pain) + enough skills/exhaust outlets exists, strongly prefer exhaust/value lines.
- Secondary heuristic: exhausting weak/status cards to reach a compact high-quality deck is good even before the full engine is online.

B. Status-to-value
- Core pieces: Power Through, Second Wind
- Scaling add-ons: Feel No Pain, Dark Embrace, Evolve, Medical Kit
- Heuristic: treat Wounds/statuses as fuel, not just liabilities, if exhaust/status synergies already exist.

C. Strength scaling
- Strength sources: Inflame, Spot Weakness, Flex, relic/potion strength
- Multipliers/payoffs: Limit Break, Heavy Blade, Sword Boomerang, Twin Strike, Whirlwind, Reaper
- Heuristic: Limit Break is premium only if reliable strength exists or is likely to exist soon.
- Headbutt/Exhume increase the value of Limit Break.

D. Block-to-damage
- Core pieces: Barricade, Entrench, Body Slam
- Support: Impervious, Power Through, Second Wind, Feel No Pain
- Heuristic: Body Slam is a win condition if block generation is already strong; Barricade raises the ceiling but is not always mandatory.

E. Thin-deck infinite
- Dropkick loop requires reliable Vulnerable application and a very thin deck.
- Sundial infinite requires thin-deck shuffle loops and enough draw efficiency.

## Noteworthy Relics
Snecko Eye: Ironclad has the most expensive card pool — average cost reduction is highest.
  Drawing 2 extra cards/turn with Confused is almost always worth it.
Dead Branch: Extremely powerful with Corruption BUT if you're already going infinite,
  Dead Branch will ruin your deck by adding random cards. Only take if NOT pursuing
  infinite combo.
Sundial: Easy infinites — exhaust deck down, cycle Pommel Strike + draw card repeatedly.
Calipers/Barricade: Only relevant if Block path — otherwise skip.
Pocketwatch: Ironclad's expensive cards make the 3-card trigger easy to hit.
 
## Common Mistakes
- Forcing an archetype the run isn't offering. Take strong standalone cards early,
  steer toward a path only once 2+ enablers appear.
- Playing Corruption without Feel No Pain or Dark Embrace. You run out of cards and die.
- Taking Demon Form without energy support. At 3 cost, it eats your entire first turn.
- Undervaluing Offering. It is almost always correct to take regardless of build.
- Taking Dead Branch when already going infinite. It breaks your loop.
- Pursuing Barricade build at high Ascension without early Barricade + block support.
- Not using exhaust as a general tool. Even without the full engine, True Grit+ and
  Burning Pact improve cycle time by removing weak cards mid-fight.
 
## Act-Specific Notes
Act 1: Frontload damage is king. Take common attacks (Pommel Strike, Carnage, Headbutt)
  to handle hallway fights and elites. Bash is your only Vulnerable source — upgrade it
  if nothing better. Path through 2-3 elites for relics if deck has frontload.
Act 2: You need scaling or you will die to the boss. Demon Form, Inflame, or the start
  of an exhaust engine. Also need AoE — Slavers and Byrds punish single-target decks.
Act 3: Deck should be tight and focused. Every card should have a job. Boss fights are
  long — you need a plan that gets stronger over 10+ turns. Reptomancer demands AoE or
  immediate dagger removal. Against Awakened One, front-load powers Turn 1 before it
  stacks Strength from your power plays.
